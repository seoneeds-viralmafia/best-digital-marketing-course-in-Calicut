Digital marketing is no longer optional for businesses. More companies are shifting budgets toward online channels because performance can be tracked and improved in real time. That also means employers want people who can plan campaigns, execute consistently, and read data like a story—what worked, what didn’t, and why.

Unlike many traditional degrees that lean heavily on theory, digital marketing is skill-driven. Hiring managers care about what you can do: keyword research, ad setup, conversion tracking, content planning, and reporting. When you build these skills, you open doors across industries—not just one niche.
